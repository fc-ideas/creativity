# Creativity Papers

This directory contains two working papers on computational theories of creativity. One focuses on creativity as a property of generative representations that support efficient local adaptation. The other frames creativity more broadly as the expansion of problem spaces through reframing, abstraction, and new operators.

## 1. [Creativity as Decomposition-Induced Evolvability in Generative Graph Models](/Users/fabriziocosta/Resilio%20Sync/Sync/Projects/EXPLORATIONS/creativity/decomposition-induced-evolvability-in-generative-graph-models.md)

Author: Fabrizio Costa. Date: 2026-04-11.

This paper defines creativity in generative models as the ability to induce decompositions that make downstream task solutions reachable through simple local substitutions. Rather than evaluating models by novelty or sample quality alone, it treats the model's decomposition as a representational theory that determines which structures are reusable, recombinable, and evolvable across external tasks.

## 2. [Towards an Operational Theory of Creativity: Reframing, Abstraction, and the Expansion of Problem-Solution Spaces](/Users/fabriziocosta/Resilio%20Sync/Sync/Projects/EXPLORATIONS/creativity/towards-an-operational-theory-of-creativity.md)

Author: Fabrizio Costa. Date: 2026-04-11.

This paper proposes a broader operational account of creativity as the transformation of a problem-solution space through new representations, abstractions, and operators. It emphasizes creativity not as novelty in isolation, but as the introduction of conceptual tools that unlock new solvable problems, improve efficiency, and propagate across domains through reuse.
